Personal Tips & Advice

About

These are some of my personal tips and advice about COVID-19. Some of the statements I make may seem biased or opinionated, so it’s okay if you don’t agree with every one of my statements. Just felt the need to share some of my thoughts about COVID-19 with you all!

Tip #1 - Viruses

It’s important to understand how most viruses (including COVID-19) work. Viruses evolve and adapt over time, meaning that scientists learn about how viruses work over time. There is so much that we don’t know about COVID-19 today, which is why it is important to stay updated and informed about the virus. Scientists around the world are working 24/7 to learn more about COVID-19, how it spreads so easily, how it originated, how to prevent it (a cure or vaccine) and much more.

Tip #2 - Public Settings

Make sure you are being mindful and respectful towards others if you choose to go out in public settings. If a store, business or restaurant requires you wear a mask or cloth face covering while inside their building, make sure you follow their guidelines out of respect for the business and the customers around you. Keep in mind that you may be asked to leave if you don’t follow store, business or restaurant policies.

Tip #3 - Misinformation

There is A LOT of misinformation about COVID-19 everywhere on the internet, ESPECIALLY on social media. I suggest relying on the eight resources I listed in the "home" page for credible and trustworthy information on COVID-19. Information is powerful. Being misinformed about COVID-19 or spreading myths and false information about the virus can cause harm towards others who are less-informed about COVID-19.


These organizations provide facts and information that debunk common myths about COVID-19:

  • The World Health Organization (WHO)

  • Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)

  • U.S. Department of Defense

  • University of Maryland Medical System

Tip #4 - Excercise

I’m sure many of you, including myself, enjoy exercising and working out. It’s important to note that you should NOT wear masks or cloth face coverings while exercising, as it can reduce your ability to breathe and cause difficulty breathing (according to the CDC). If you choose to wear a mask or cloth face covering, do it at your own risk.

Tip #5 - School

If you are a college student like me or a person currently in school, it’s important to remain updated on any new policies that your school may put in place. Since a lot of schools plan to open in Fall 2020, it’s important to remain updated on any information your school provides about how they will be handling COVID-19.

Tip #6 - Hospitalization

Not everyone who tests positive for COVID-19 needs to be hospitalized. Unless you develop severe symptoms or illness from COVID-19, it's best that you treat yourself at home rather than at the hospital (which may be overcrowded depending on where you live). Understand that priority access to hospitals for COVID-19 goes to people with severe symptoms or illness from COVID-19.

Tip #7 - Mental Health

Mental health is important, especially at a time where the world is in the midst of a global pandemic. You may have lost a loved one, lost a job, feel isolated or alone, have loved ones working on the front lines of healthcare or essential businesses and more. You are certainly not alone, and it is important to know that millions of people are in the same situation as you. The CDC and WHO provide excellent resources on coping with mental health during the COVID-19 global pandemic.

Tip #8 - Take a break!

Take a break from your devices or social media, take a walk outside, read a book, exercise, try to learn a new skill, meditate, listen to music or podcasts or do any other activity that will help relax your mind.

Tip #9 - Communication

Communication is one of the biggest barriers that many people, including myself, face during this difficult time. Make sure that you’re doing your best to regularly communicate with your family members and loved ones, whether through a call, FaceTime, Zoom, WebEx meeting, etc. COVID-19 has made it difficult for people to have normal in-person events and activities, and has shifted many things to an online and virtual setting. There may be some family members, loved ones or friends who we haven’t seen in person for a while, so you make sure you try your best to communicate with them as much as possible!

Tip #10 - Do your part!

Do your part! Try to follow as many preventive measures as you can when leaving your home and entering into a public setting. Slowing the spread of COVID-19 takes the combined effort of EVERYONE. I believe that life will get back to normal if everyone plays their part in the effort to slow the spread of COVID-19 and hopefully stop the spread in its tracks.

To anyone reading this, I hope you stay healthy, informed and blessed!
